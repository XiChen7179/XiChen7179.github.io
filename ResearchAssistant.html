    Georgia Tech Autonomous Connection and Transporation Lab

    My RA Experience as a SubTeam Leader

    Background

    Georgia Tech Autonomous Connection and Transportation Lab is at the forefront of pioneering solutions for tomorrow's mobility challenges in the smart cities. Within this broad mission, my specific subteam—the Human-Machine Interface (HMI) group—aims to bridge the gap between human users and autonomous systems. Our goal is to create intuitive, user-friendly interfaces that facilitate interaction between people and machines, ultimately contributing to the widespread acceptance and success of autonomous transportation technologies.
    Coupled with my academic background in intelligence and info networking and my commitment to fostering a collaborative team environment, these qualities distinguished me as a prime candidate. Following a thorough evaluation by the lab's senior leadership, which included assessments of my technical contributions, leadership abilities, and a proposal for the HMI group's future research direction, I was entrusted with the leadership of the HMI group.

    HMI Project

    This HMI interface is designed to enhance vehicular safety and situational awareness by displaying real-time data, such as vehicle coordinates, and deploying stop sign alerts when necessary. It ensures drivers and autonomous systems are well-informed and responsive to dynamic road conditions, fostering a safer driving environment.

    Cyber-Detection Project

    This research introduces a novel intrusion detection system for the Controller Area Network (CAN) in vehicles, aimed at enhancing security against cyber attacks.
