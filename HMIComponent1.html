    ACT Lab: Human Machine Interface Components

    Stop Sign and Real-time Data Transportation

    Description

    The platform functions as a vital support system for the driving experience, presenting real-time information about the vehicle and issuing alerts when necessary.

    Problem it solves

    In the context of self-driving vehicles and Vehicle-to-Vehicle (V2V) communication, the platform addresses the critical need for dynamic, real-time data sharing and processing. It solves the problem of ensuring that autonomous vehicles remain constantly informed about their immediate environment, including the status of nearby vehicles, to enhance safety and efficiency on the road. By providing timely alerts and updates, the platform aids in preventing potential accidents, optimizing traffic flow, and facilitating seamless V2V interactions, crucial for the effective operation of self-driving systems.

    UDP Protocol

    This feature allows for fast, efficient communication between vehicles and the system by utilizing UDP, a lightweight communication protocol. This integration is crucial for transmitting real-time vehicle data, such as coordinates and other sensor information, with minimal latency.

    Unity Interface

    This feature enables the realistic rendering of vehicles and their surroundings, providing a comprehensive view of real-time vehicle data and alerts, such as stop signs.
